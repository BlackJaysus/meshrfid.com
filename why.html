RFID is powerful, but most businesses can’t use it properly.

Everyone wants to know what stock they own, where it is and what it’s worth. RFID can do that, but the way it is sold makes it hard to justify.

  • Upfront hardware and integration costs that feel more like a new ERP than a tool.
  • Multiple systems – WMS, ERP, handhelds, spreadsheets – that never quite agree.
  • Data that is technically impressive but doesn’t help decide what to buy, move or write off.
MeshRFID starts from the business reality: limited capital, busy floors, and stock that changes daily. The goal is not “RFID everywhere”. It is “reliable numbers that anyone can work with”.

How RFID normally looks.

The classic RFID project is heavy: months of planning, consultants and new kit before a single useful count reaches finance.

  • Consultants, discovery workshops and IT project plans before any tags go on product.
  • Dedicated terminals and readers that only a handful of people know how to use.
  • Static reports that don’t tie neatly back to stock values on the balance sheet.
If you need a new steering committee and a slide deck to move from pilot to rollout, the bar is too high. That is why so many RFID projects stall after the first phase.

What MeshRFID changes.

MeshRFID treats RFID as a practical tool, not a capital project. The focus is on simple workflows and clear numbers instead of impressive infrastructure.

  • Runs on smartphones and simple readers your team can handle on day one.
  • Slots in alongside your current WMS, ERP and spreadsheets instead of replacing them.
  • Surfaces stock, value and ageing in a way operators and finance can both read in minutes.
Start with a single problem: a warehouse, an outlet or one client’s stock. Prove that shrink falls and decisions improve. Only then scale to more sites.

Who MeshRFID is designed for
